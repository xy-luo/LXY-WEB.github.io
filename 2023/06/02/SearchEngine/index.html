1. Search Engine Define / Concepts

Web search engine: a software system that designed to search for information on the Wrold Wide Web.
*Search engine often maintain real-time information by running an algorithm on a web crawler.

SERPs: Search engine results pages.

2. Search Engine Optimization(SEO)

2.1 跟SEO相关概念：How google search works

  1. Crawling: Google navidates the web by crawling
  2. Indexing: Google analyzes the text, images, and video files on the page, and stores the information in the Google index, which is a large database(from documents(webpages) to structured data/index).
  3. Serving search results: When a user searches on Google, Google returns information that’s relevant to the user’s query.
    refer: https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/how-search-works

3. Search Engine Marketing(SEM)

谁应该出现在广告位上？

3.1 SEM相关概念

Google uses a bid-based PPC model that encourages advertisers to bid against each other for coveted keywords.
*A keyword is a word or phrase that matches what people are Googling.
Targeted Keywords, every ad targets certain keywords

3.1.1 Ad Rank Calculation

Google AdRank - relevance score(high - more relevant, low - less relevant)
The value of the score used to determine ad position and whether your ads will show at all.

Ad Rank is calculated by:

  1. Bid
  2. The quality of ads and landing page
  3. Ad Rank Thresholds
  4. The context of person’s search
  5. The expected impact from your ad extensions and other ad formats

terminology Explanation

CPC(Cost Per Click):
PPC(Pay Per Click): is a digital advertising model, where advertisers are charged every time their ad is clicked.

  • bid: the maximum amount you’re willing to pay for a click on your ad. (The actually end up paying is often less, and the bid can be changed at any time)
  • Quality of ads and landing page: how relevant and useful your ad and the website it links to are to the person who’ll see it. The assessment of the quality of ad is summarized in Quality Score.
  • Ad Rank Thresholds: Help ensure high quality ads, we set minimum thresholds that an ad must achieve to show in a particular ad position.
  • The context of person’s search: the search terms the person has entered, the person’s location at the time of the search, the type of device they’re using, the time of the search, the nature of the search terms, other ads and search results that show on the page, and other user signals and attributes.
  • The expected impact from your ad extensions and other ad formats: When create ad, you have the option to add additional information to your ad, such as a phone number, or more links to specific pages on your site. All of these are called ad extensions. AdWords estimates how extensions and other ad formats you use will impact your ad’s performance.

3.1.2 Auction(Bid)

Bidders submit written bids without knowing the bid of the other people in the auction.

  1. Fist Price Auction
    The highest bidder wins and end up paying the price he bid.
    cons:
    1. The bidders might bid with relatively lower price because they are afraid of paying too much exceeding the value of the products.
    2. In this case, the winner might feel cheated when he finds the second perosn was only valuing the item way lower then he paid.
  2. Vickrey Auction
    The highest bidder wins but the price paid is the second-highest bid.
